# Visualization Design Principles

## Dashboard Design Explanation

The dashboard was designed for a leadership audience to provide a clear overview of business performance while supporting data-driven decision-making. Appropriate visualization techniques were selected to highlight trends, comparisons, and geographic performance without unnecessary complexity.

---

## Correct Chart Selection

Different chart types were selected based on the business question being answered.

* A **line chart** was used to show changes in sales over time.
* **Bar charts** were used to compare regional performance, category profitability, and shipping performance.
* A **scatter plot** was used to examine the relationship between discount and profit.
* A **map** was used to visualize sales distribution across states.
* **KPI cards** summarize Total Sales, Total Profit, and Profit Margin for quick performance assessment.

---

## Clear Hierarchy

The dashboard follows a logical layout. Key performance indicators are placed at the top to provide an immediate business summary. Trend and performance charts are positioned in the middle, while detailed regional and geographic analysis is displayed below, allowing users to move naturally from high-level insights to detailed information.

---

## Minimal Clutter

Only the most relevant charts and business metrics are included. Unnecessary gridlines, labels, legends, and decorative elements were minimized to improve readability and ensure the dashboard remains easy to interpret.

---

## Consistent Color Usage

A consistent colour scheme is used throughout the dashboard. Similar colours represent similar measures, making comparisons easier and improving the overall visual consistency of the dashboard.

---

## Proper Labels

Every visualization includes clear titles and axis labels where required. KPI values are clearly labelled so that users can understand each metric without additional explanation.

---

## Readable Titles

Worksheet titles such as **Sales Trend View**, **Regional Performance View**, **Category Profitability View**, **Shipping Performance View**, and **State Sales** clearly describe the purpose of each visualization and improve navigation within the dashboard.

---

## Appropriate Sorting

Bar charts are sorted based on business performance, allowing leadership to quickly identify the highest and lowest performing regions, categories, and shipping methods.

---

## Useful Filters

Interactive filters such as Region, Category, Customer Segment, and Ship Mode allow users to focus on specific business areas and perform detailed analysis without changing the overall dashboard structure.

---

## Avoidance of Misleading Scales

Charts use consistent axes and appropriate scales to ensure values are represented accurately. No visual exaggeration or truncated axes are used, allowing fair comparisons between business metrics.

---

## Focus on Business Interpretation

The dashboard is designed to answer important business questions rather than simply displaying data. It enables leadership to monitor sales trends, evaluate regional and category performance, assess profitability, understand shipping efficiency, and identify opportunities for improving business performance through data-driven decisions.
